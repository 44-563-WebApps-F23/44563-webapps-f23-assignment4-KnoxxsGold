Brady Nelson acts in a play

Ghosts by Henrik Ibsen

MRS. ALVING. Yes; of course he had to take the blame upon himself. MANDERS. But such a piece of duplicity on his part! And towards me too! I never could have believed it of Jacob Engstrand. I shall not fail to take him seriously to task; he may be sure of that.--And then the immorality of such a connection! For money--! How much did the girl receive? MRS. ALVING. Three hundred dollars. MANDERS. Just think of it--for a miserable three hundred dollars, to go and marry a fallen woman! MRS. ALVING. Then what have you to say of me? I went and married a fallen man. MANDERS. Why--good heavens!--what are you talking about! A fallen man! MRS. ALVING. Do you think Alving was any purer when I went with him to the altar than Johanna was when Engstrand married her?
